Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos, Nigeria, is the most populous city in Africa and a major global megacity located along the Gulf of Guinea. Historically known as Eko by the indigenous Yoruba people, it served as Nigeria's national capital until 1991, when the seat of government moved to Abuja. Today, the city remains the country's undisputed economic powerhouse, accounting for roughly 25–30% of Nigeria's GDP and hosting the continent's busiest seaports in Apapa and Tin Can Island
